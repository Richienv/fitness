// 04 — Discipline
DO / DON'T

✓ DO

🍳3 eggs every morning. No exceptions, even weekends, even if you're lazy. Cook them in 5 minutes.
💊Creatine 10g every day. Including rest days. Consistency matters more than timing.
🎒Bring gym bag to school. Go straight from class. Don't go home first — you won't come back out.
💧3–4L water daily. Creatine requires water to work. Dehydration = no pump, no strength.
📊Log your weights mentally. Remember what you lifted last session. Beat it this session.
🛌7–8 hours sleep. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Late nights are killing your gains.
🍌Eat bananas on gym days. Your calorie gap is real. Bananas close it fast.
📸Weekly photo every Sunday morning. Mirror lies — photos don't. This is your real data.
⏱️Rest 60–90 sec between sets. Not 3 minutes on your phone. Set a timer.
🔄3 second negative on every rep. Especially lat pulldown, bicep curl. Slow down = more muscle.

✗ DON'T

🍚Don't eat rice. Your rule, your goal. Bananas and veggies are your carbs. Stick to it.
📱Don't scroll between sets. You lose rest timing and mental focus. Music yes, scrolling no.
🏠Don't go home before gym. You will not go back out. This is a known pattern — don't test it.
😴Don't skip breakfast. Skipping = 155g protein becomes impossible. The whole day collapses.
⚖️Don't weigh yourself daily. Water fluctuations will mess with your head. Sundays only.
🚫Don't train injured muscles. Pain ≠ gains. Soreness is fine. Sharp or joint pain = stop immediately.
🍟Don't eat junk to fill calorie gap. The gap is real but fill it with bananas, eggs, peanut butter — not fried food.
🏃Don't add cardio "to burn fat." Your cycling is enough. Extra cardio = more calories needed = harder to maintain muscle.
💪Don't ego-lift. Wrong form with heavy weight builds nothing. 8 reps clean > 5 reps ugly.
🍽️Don't under-eat on gym days. 1,200 kcal on a training day = muscle breakdown. You did this today — never again.
// 05 — Timeline
WHAT TO EXPECT

Week by week — realistic expectations. No hype, no lies.

// Week 1–2
Soreness & Calibration
You will be sore. Especially legs after Day 3, and back after Pull A. This is normal — DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness) peaks 24–48 hours after training. Don't skip the next session because you're sore — training sore muscles with lighter weight actually helps recovery. Scale might go up 0.5–1kg from creatine pulling water into muscles. This is NOT fat gain. You will not look different yet. Focus only on habits.
// Week 3–4
Strength Jumps, First Signs
Soreness decreases dramatically. You'll notice you can lift more than week 1 — this is neuromuscular adaptation (your brain getting better at recruiting muscle). Pump in the gym feels real now. In the mirror, shoulders might look slightly fuller. Face stays the same. Waist possibly 0.5cm tighter. This week you take your first progress photo Sunday morning — you probably won't see much but it's the baseline that matters at week 12.
// Week 5–6
The Turn — People Start Noticing
This is the first week it becomes real. Actual muscle protein synthesis is compounding. Shoulders look rounder and wider from the front. When you wear a fitted shirt, the shoulder-to-waist silhouette is different. Clothes fit differently. Renita might comment. You'll look at your back in the gym mirror and see lats emerging. Scale stays 70–74kg — good, this means you're gaining muscle while losing fat (recomp working). Energy throughout the day is higher.
// Week 7–8
Potential Fatigue + Mid-program Plateau
This is the hardest mental week. Progress feels slower. Some lifts might stall. You might feel more tired than usual. This is normal — your body is under cumulative stress from 7–8 weeks of hard training. Do NOT cut calories here. Eat at 2,200 kcal on all gym days. Sleep 8 hours every night this week. If everything hurts — take an extra rest day. One extra rest day is worth more than training with 60% effort. If you push through week 7–8, week 9 will feel electric.
// Week 9–10
Second Strength Spike + Abs Appear
Upper abs visible in good lighting, flexed. Possibly visible relaxed if body fat has dropped. Lateral raise and face pull volume is now paying off — shoulder width is notably wider than month 1. Strength on lat pulldown, seated row, bench all significantly above starting point. You carry yourself differently — posture is better because lats and rear delts are actually developed now. Face still sharp — this is the key indicator that body fat is where it should be.
// Week 11–12
V-Taper Unlocked
This is the transformation payoff week. V-taper clearly visible from the front — wide shoulders, narrow waist. Lats visible through a T-shirt from behind. Upper abs visible at rest in normal lighting. Arms look fuller and more defined. Face is sharp — jaw more defined. When you take the week 12 photo next to the week 1 photo, the difference will be undeniable. Shoulder:waist ratio should be approaching or exceeding 1.45:1. You are now in shape. The next 12-week cycle builds on this foundation with heavier weights and more volume.
Most important thing to remember: Progress is not linear. There will be weeks you feel flat, weeks you feel invincible. Trust the process, hit your protein, don't miss sessions. The transformation at week 12 is a mathematical certainty if you execute consistently. The only variable is you.
